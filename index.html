Full Stack QA Certified Professional (FSQCP)

If you know, a QA professional working on all aspects of quality and applying different testing methods to test their software, is called a full-stack QA professional artist. A full-stack QA professional will be comfortable working across all of the application's stages, and will be familiar with wide parts of its functionality.

What is Full Stack QA?

This term is generally used from an application developer’s perspective e.g. a full-stack developer – professional who can do both front end and back end coding or individual who is keen understanding of all the front end and back end technologies.
The similar concept is applicable to Quality Assurance Engineers. It would describe an all-rounder who can perform and maintain various types or different aspects of testing such as manual testing, automation testing, security testing, and performance testing, and so on.

What are the benefits of Full Stack QA certification?

There are several benefits related to continuous learning with this course certification role, an individual has a chance to learn multiple technologies or tools. As explained earlier, a full-stack QA professional will be responsible for both manual and automation testing. If you look at the skills mentioned in this course, you can understand what type of understanding and capability a full-stack QA professional is required to have. For such a wide range of skillsets, you need to keep learning new skills available in the market at regular intervals and keep yourself upgrade with necessity and requirement.
Due to the multiple skills requirements from a single resource, you always get an ideal salary option in company point of view. Companies will be ready to pay more money for an ideal aspirants.
Hence, full-stack QAs are better-paid specialist QA or Test Automation Engineers.

Why we should learn Full Stack QA?

The prominent part is why we should learn it? The primary motivation for full-stack QA is the adoption of agile product development processes. As software updates are delivered more instantly, QA professionals have less time to complete all the testing. There's not much time towards the end of a sprint, and it’s certainly too late to do it months after the feature’s development is ‘complete’ (can it really be accomplish if you’ve not finished testing part?!) The QA professional must grow their skills to match the new technology advancement, testing techniques and methodologies currently in vogue. A respected QA professional with a range of skillsets can command a high paid salary, enjoy job satisfaction and a wonderful career.

FULL STACK QA CERTIFIED PROFESSIONAL FAQs


•How do I prepare for a full stack interview?

As a full stack developer, you should be adept at front-end development that uses Java, JavaScript, and HTML. The knowledge of the Ruby program is also desirable. In addition, in-depth knowledge of palindrome functions and data structures will ensure you go a long way in the interview process.

•What comes in full stack development?

A full stack web developer is a person who can develop both client and server software. In addition to mastering HTML and CSS, he/she also knows how to: Program a browser (like using JavaScript, jQuery, Angular, or Vue) Program a server (like using PHP, ASP, Python, or Node)

• What is SDET engineer?

An SDET, in layman terms, is a developer who instead of working in the product development team, works as part of the test team. In essence, SDETs are responsible not only for writing code but are required to test the code as well. SDETs are required to continuously write, test, and fix the written code.

•What is difference between QA and QC?

QA primarily focuses on the processes and procedures that improve quality, including training, documentation, monitoring and audits. QC focuses on the product to find defects that remain after development. QC professionals find these issues in a variety of ways, including software testing and beta or canary testing.

•Can QA become full stack developer?

Yes it's possible to be a full stack QA. A full-stack QA engineer is comfortable working across all of the application layers, and will be familiar with large parts of its functionality. They will think about the many different aspects of product quality, such as functionality, usability, performance, security etc.

•What does a full stack QA engineer do?

The full-stack QA engineer will work with the developers to understand a feature's architecture, how it's implemented, and the technologies used. This will help them determine how best to test the feature. Consider, for example, auto-complete on a selection list.

•Is SDET and QE same?

A QE is not a developer who focuses on testing (such as an SDET), nor is it someone who focuses on development and operations (such as a DevOps), nor is it someone who ensures quality (like a QA).

• Is QA Engineer same as tester?

Tester's main focus is to test the application and find all the possible bugs which could crash the application. While on the other hand, a QA Engineer follows a set of methods and activities designed to ensure that the software application is as per standards or SRS and corresponds to all the specifications.

• Do QA engineers use SQL?

After learning manual QA and test automation, QA engineer should acquire the database and SQL knowledge. Hence, we dedicated this space to outline the top SQL stuff that can immensely help the test engineers. For DBA, SQL skill is far more important as they have to pursue this skill for carrying out the routine tasks.

• What is the job of a QA analyst?

The Quality Assurance (QA) Analyst will conduct testing on software, websites, and other technical products to identify and resolve bugs, defects, and other potential issues.
